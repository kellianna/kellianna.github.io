02 Chapter Two

So far everything I've told you is reality. Not the shifting reality of falling asleep or staring out the window at nothing, but the real story. I can even tell you that the lead singer who put his hands between my legs and asked if it was ok was named Jay. And he had amazing hair and applied his eyeliner with more care than I did my own. I can tell you that college sucked for me because I reverted to the quiet writer who lingered in the library drinking coffee at midnight and reading Virginia Woolf because I thought that's what I should be doing. I had followed Dave's lead from one thing to another and had never really chosen an identity of my own. Was I writing to gain Daddy's love? Or to feel that feeling of the way words wound around my throat and erupted in raw emotion like they had on stage? I didn't know because I never asked. I was the shadow of my older brother, I was a ghost, a personality thin enough to see through even if my body had worn a size XL school girl skirt with my fishnets.

Dave graduated and started working for a record company as an unpaid intern, which meant he was also working as a cashier at a grocery store to pay the bills. School and life pulled us apart and for the first time I was rudderless, unmoored without my big brother to guide and protect me. I graduated a year later and got a job offer to write for a pop culture website. The internet was still relatively new and the opportunity was too good to pass up, even if the job was in Milwaukee. Further away from everything that grounded me, I set up a home in a tiny apartment just outside of the city and took the bus to an even tinier office everyday. The other women working at the website were beauty experts or die hard fans of the popular bands that they played on the "adult" listening stations. Not one of them had ever heard of Henry Rollins or The Misfits. Not one of them had ever been a misfit. Not one of them had ever pulled their stockings down to let a boy who's leather jacket smelled like beer and sweat inside them in a tiny backroom while a cover of "Ball and Chain" blasted outside the door. And not one of them would have believed that I had done that very thing. The fat girl who handled the section of the website that focused on best selling books. Mostly self help and romances - you can see the demographic.

You've probably noticed that I brought up this tryst with Jay three times already in just the first chapter and a half. That's mostly because it was all the sex I had for many years after. It wasn't just that one time either. There was an actual hotel room in Madison where we slept wrapped around each other after. Where I woke up to green eyes staring into mine and his hand on my hip. Soon to be on my knees taking him into my mouth while he moaned my name and I believed in love songs all over again. But since then, I had just been the quiet college literature student who flew under the radar and didn't invite either scorn or interest from anyone.

Three things happened in perfect synchronicity around this time. I started writing a novel, I got a job at a proper magazine (as a book reviewer, I seemed to have accidentally fallen into a niche career), and my dad reached out to both Dave and me. He was washed up. His most recent wife had left him and he was feeling the years. Dave flat out refused to see him and encouraged me to return the letters unopened. For the first time in our lives, Dave and I were on opposite ends of a situation. My instinct was to listen to my big brother who had always protected me, but I also had a big empty hole where my dad would fit perfectly. As serendipity would have it, my dad was in Seattle and that's where the magazine was headquartered. Plus, my novel needed a setting, why not the Pacific Northwest?

This is where you think the story starts. This is where you're thinking that the backstory is finally over and we can get on with whatever the fuck this book is supposed to be about. I wish it were that simple. I wish I could tell you that yes, this is where the story really gets going. Where I got in touch with my dad, love my job and Seattle and I lived happily ever after. Well, that wouldn't make much of a novel, now would it?

No. Things kind of went of the rails in a place known for rain and the birth of Nirvana. I had resisted grunge for a long time after it killed off my beloved punk, but I had come around by the time I found myself in Seattle. By now Kurt was dead and there was something post grunge about the whole scene, but that didn't stop me from immersing myself in the music scene. Maybe throwing on a hippie dress and leaving it on the floor of some drummer's apartment overnight. I found that drummers had the best rhythm when it came to tongues on certain body parts. it was the drummers that would have me rocking my hips and begging for more. But, none of it went anywhere. And neither did the novel.

I was trying to write in between club nights, book reviews, and raindrops and there wasn't enough coffee in Seattle to power that. The story was about a popular girl who found out her group of friends were all aliens underneath perfect faces and hair. My plan for the ending was that the girl was an alien too, but it was just as awful as it sounds. I would write and rewrite, but there was no saving what was a horrible idea from the beginning. I was writing amazing book reviews for the magazine, maybe I should have made note of what worked in the novels I loved most.

But, you're waiting to hear about my reunion with my dad. You don't care about awful novels and drummers with shoulder tattoos and amazing tongues. The story doesn't really start there either, but at least it went better than my novel.
