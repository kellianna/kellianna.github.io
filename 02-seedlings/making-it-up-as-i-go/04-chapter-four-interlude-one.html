04 Chapter Four - Interlude One

There are many different directions I could take you at this point. I want you to stop and think about that for just a moment. In any novel the author can decide to take you on many different paths. It feels, for you the reader, like there was only one way that story could have gone. The book you read is exactly how things happened, but they never happened so there's no reason they couldn't have happened in a different way. You have no idea how many decisions the author made, how many times they changed a scene, how different the first draft may have been. The sign of a good story, a good author, is that you feel like the fictional characters are real, that you are reading the story of an actual event. As I keep breaking the fourth wall, I am obviously not that author. I just want you to understand how this story is a bit like the theory of the multiverse. There are so many discarded realities that I could go with at what I have decided is now the beginning of the story.

I could tell you that I went back to my hometown, fell in love with a simple, common man and realized that I didn't need all the glitz and glamor of a minor celebrity adjacent existence. I actually wrote this story once before and it sucked like a made for TV romcom. So, that's not our reality.

I could tell you that it was here I learned that I was a "child of air." Someone who when presented with a special stone from my spiritual guide could travel through the multiverse trying to find the right path to make the world a better place. That my brother traveled with me (we were named Shelley and Byron in that story because of my love of the romantics) and there were others like us who just tried to find the right solution to the world's problems. In the end we would learn that it was art and creativity. Art and creativity that actually brought about lasting societal change. This is another novel I started once and abandoned. It's really somewhat cliche, so that's not our reality either.

I could tell you that it was here that a bomb was dropped. That much of the world's water was poisoned and we were thrown into nuclear winter. That my ex-husband, children, and brother died of the radiation, and I went slowly mad with grief. Until I stumbled upon a small community forming a resistance to the government who had risen up in the aftermath of the bombs. A small community making change through, you guessed it, art and creativity. I found my sanity and we saved the world through art. This is a story I was going to tell through poetry, like a concept album. But, really, it's just another cliche isn't it? No, that's not our reality.

I bet you are growing as frustrated with this as I am. Learning that every idea is just a recycling of an old idea. Every story is based on a cliche. Why even bother to read anymore when you've read every story out there twenty times already, just slightly altered in form and setting?

Because that's our fate. As readers and writers it is our fate to take a story told again and again and somehow make it our own as it's recycled through the machine of our own experience and knowledge base. We could both read the same book and see it in entirely different ways. That's why my jobs as a book reviewer were bullshit. I would write a pan of a book and yet someone else out there may have found themselves in that story. Or maybe it was just a story that the author needed to tell. Reviews have their place, but they also get in the way of people finding their true loves.

So, here we are. All the old ideas laid out and cast aside. They are not our universe, our reality. They are graves in the cemetery of ideas. Let them rest. Let them lie. Let's be bold and forge ahead. I could have told you this story from my twenties. I could have expanded on my marriage and my life as a mom. I could have told this very story from any place we have already been. But it wasn't what this story wanted to be. This story wanted a new hero. It didn't want a fresh faced twenty year old. It didn't want a struggling new mother or a woman on the cusp of forty.

This story wants to break at least one cliche. Allow a fifty year old woman a new beginning. So, let me tell you where I went from there.
