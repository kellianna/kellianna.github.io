05 Chapter Five

I was crushing hard on my neighbor. I thought that at fifty, I had left this side of me behind in the clubs of Seattle. The last drummer (Who would later go on to be a drummer for two pretty major bands, but as he was the drummer, no one really knew his name. Yet another minor celebrity encounter.) had fucked me in a janitor's closet and then called me a fat ass and told me to leave. It was the rare time that I didn't feel good about embracing my sexual freedom. I was always glad I had gotten married after that and became a respectable mother. Family gave me a place to do penance for my past sins. Every blown out diaper, all night crying spell, violent stomach flu that needed to be cleaned from the carpet, every night when my actor husband didn't come home and I was left with a teenager heartbroken over an ending friendship, was another bead on the rosary of forgiveness. It was another Hail Mary toward being a "good" person. In the end all I ended up was a non-person. Someone with a past she tried to hide and late night panic attacks that I had made a fool of myself for decades. So, to find myself lusting after my neighbor in my new life, was quite a surprise.

My new apartment was small. There were two bedrooms and a pullout couch so the kids could come home, but really, it was tiny. It reminded me of when I was living in Milwaukee and working at the website all those years ago. But now I could appreciate having a space of my own. A space not taken up by scripts and mountain bikes, school books and backpacks. My mom had been nice enough to let me store boxes of memorabilia at her house so I still had some of the kid's old clothes and toys, but my apartment was unapologetically my own. My oldest had a good job in Toronto as a linguist. My youngest was about to graduate and start a master's degree in politics. They had their own lives and didn't need a shrine to their younger selves in their mother's home. A few pictures, some children's books they loved. It was enough to keep them close.

Otherwise, it was earth tones and floor to ceiling books. A desk with a laptop where I was struggling to reclaim my voice. At night there were the faint strains of music coming from the apartment next door, if I wandered out onto my tiny balcony, I could hear the music even louder. Strains of Pearl Jam, Prince, Tracey Chapman, and what really hooked me, Rancid. Although I think the night I decided to knock on my neighbor's door, it was Lionel Richie singing "Stuck on You" that lead me like a cartoon dog floating through the air after a scent.

I didn't knock on the door with any pretense in mind. I wasn't there to ask said neighbor to turn down the music or to borrow a cup of sugar. I just had to see the person who's music so often exactly matched my mood. I slipped out into the hall in my sock feet and knocked at the door. After a few seconds it was opened by a guy around my age, dark blue t-shirt, kind brown eyes, tasteful mustache that made me realize I had never kissed a man with a mustache before. I was so distracted by this idea that I didn't hear him say hello until he asked a little more loudly if he could help me.

"Oh, right, hi," I stammered. "I'm your neighbor next door and I was just coming by to..."

"Ask me to turn down the music? I know it's probably too loud, but your apartment was empty for months and I got kind of used to being alone on this end of the hall." He looked at me with a sheepish grin that tugged at something that hadn't been tugged in a long time.

"No, no. It was the music that made me come over, but more to compliment your taste than to ask you to turn it down."

I bowed my head in what I hoped was a shy, flirty move and realized I had knocked on this man's door in pajama pants and an oversized Foo Fighters sweatshirt that had seen better days in the early 2000s. I hadn't really prepared to meet anyone, I hadn't really thought this through.

"Compliment me?" he asked confused by my rusty social skills.

"The music. I've been enjoying the songs in your rotation. It's trippy how often they match my mood."

He visibly relaxed and exhaled, "That's so awesome. I was really worried I had been a bad neighbor. Although, I should have come over and introduced myself sooner. I'm Carlos." He reached out a hand and I noticed a tiny spiral tattoo between his thumb and index finger.

"It's nice to meet you. I'm Beth." (Of course I was Beth. Remember my father's obsession with thinking he was a roadie for Kiss? Dave's just lucky his mother didn't allow our dad to name him Gene.) I took his hand in mine and felt swallowed by the size and like I was caught in that spiral for just a moment.

"Would you like to come in? I was just about to play something a little newer. Maybe some John Legend?"

The mention of John Legend snapped me back to reality. That felt like music a man put on to lull a woman into staring into his eyes while he snuck an arm around her waist and slowly tucked her under him before she knew what hit her. And I was a long way for being ready to be tucked under anyone right now.

"Maybe another time. I still have work to do on an article that's due tomorrow to my editor. I'll take a rain check on the John Legend for tonight. It was nice to meet you though. Finally."

He looked at me and I swear steam was rising off of his shoulders and his gaze and for a moment I hesitated and thought maybe I could be tucked under someone after all, but then he smiled, gave me a little wave and shut his door.

I breathed out. And tried to remember if I had put my vibrator in my nightstand. I really hoped the batteries weren't dead.
